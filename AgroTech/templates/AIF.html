Agriculture Infrastructure Fund

Scheme duration

The duration of the Scheme shall be from FY2020 to FY2032 (10 years).

Intended beneficiaries

  • Agricultural Produce Market Committee
  • Agri-Entrepreneur
  • Central sponsored Public-Private Partnership Project
  • Farmer
  • Farmer Producers Organization
  • Federation of Farmer Produce Organisations
  • Joint Liability Groups
  • Local Body sponsored Public-Private Partnership Project
  • Marketing Cooperative Society
  • Multipurpose Cooperative Society
  • National Federations of Cooperatives
  • Primary Agricultural Credit Society
  • Self Help Group
  • Federations of Self Help Groups
  • Start-Up
  • State Agencies
  • State Federations of Cooperatives
  • State sponsored Public-Private Partnership Project

Benefits

All loans under this financing facility will have interest subvention of 3% per annum up to a limit of Rs. 2 crore. This subvention will be available for a maximum period of seven years.

Further, credit guarantee coverage will be available for eligible borrowers from this financing facility under Credit Guarantee Fund Trust for Micro and Small Enterprises (CGTMSE) scheme for a loan up to Rs. 2 crore. The fee for this coverage will be paid by the Government.

In case of FPOs the credit guarantee may be availed from the facility created under FPO promotion scheme of Department of Agriculture, Cooperation & Farmers Welfare (DACFW).

Eligible State Agencies

  • State Agricultural Marketing Boards
  • State Cooperative Banks
  • State Cooperative Marketing Federations
  • State Cooperative Processing Federation
  • State Agriculture/Horticulture/Animal Husbandry/Fisheries Departments
  • State Food and Civil Supplies Departments
  • State Rural Development Departments
  • State Horticulture Mission/Board/Corporation
  • State Livestock Development Boards
  • State Fisheries Departments
  • State Agribusiness Corporations

How to Apply

Interested beneficiaries can apply for the Agriculture Infrastructure Fund by submitting their loan proposal to the eligible lending institutions or State Agencies mentioned above. The loan proposal should include the project details, cost estimation, and other relevant information as per the guidelines of the Scheme.

The lending institutions or State Agencies will evaluate the loan proposal and provide the necessary assistance for availing the benefits under the Agriculture Infrastructure Fund scheme.

Conclusion

The Agriculture Infrastructure Fund is a significant initiative by the Government of India to boost the agricultural infrastructure and support the farmers and other stakeholders in the agriculture sector. The benefits of interest subvention and credit guarantee coverage provide a much-needed financial assistance to promote investment in agriculture and allied activities. Eligible State Agencies play a crucial role in implementing the scheme and supporting the beneficiaries in availing the benefits. Interested beneficiaries can apply for the scheme by submitting their loan proposal to the eligible lending institutions or State Agencies and take advantage of the financial support provided under the Agriculture Infrastructure Fund.
